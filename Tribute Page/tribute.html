Tribute to Albert Einstein




“The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing.”

Biography

Albert Einstein (14 March 1879 – 18 April 1955) was more than a brilliant scientist.He was a symbol of curiosity, imagination, and courage to question the obvious. With ideas that reshaped our understanding of space, time, and the universe, he proved that a single curious mind can change the world. Beyond his genius, Einstein stood for peace, humility, and the joy of learning. His legacy reminds us that imagination is just as powerful as knowledge, and that asking “why” can light up the future.



Revolutionized Physics:

  • Introduced special relativity(1905), explaining Time dilation and length contraction.

  • Developed general relativity(1915), describing gravity as spacetime curvature.

Contributions:

  • Explained Photoelectric effect (1905), showing light's particle nature.

  • Predicted gravitatioal waves (1915), later confirmed by LIGO(2015).

  • Proposed the concept of black holes and wormholes.

Discovery of E = mc²:

  • Derived the mass-energy equivalence equation (1905), a fundamental concept in physics.

  • Showed that mass and energy are interchangeable.

Awards and Recognitions:

  • Nobel Prize in Physics (1921) for explaining the photoelectric effect.

  • Cordero Medal (1925) and Max Planck Medal (1929).

Humanitarian and Philosopher:

  • Advocate for peace, civil rights, and nuclear disarmament

  • Supported Zionism and Jewish causes

  • Writings on philosophy, politics, and social issues

Legacy:

  • Continues to influence science, philosophy, and culture.

  • Inspires new generations of scientists, thinkers, and innovators.
